ARTICLE                 INFO                              ABSTRACT

Keywords:                                                 Background: The inter-domain routing security is often based on trust, which, as seen in practice, is an
Routing protocols                                         insufficient approach. Due to the deficit of native security controls in the Border Gateway Protocol (BGP),
Network security                                          many new routing security measures were proposed to prevent control-plane abuse. They must be implemented
Telecommunication network reliability
                                                          in the majority of the Internet’s Autonomous Systems.
Wide area networks
                                                          Objective: This study undertakes a scoping review of the routing security domain to provide the most up-
                                                          to-date and state-of-the-art broad summary of threat classification, prevention, and mitigation. The authors
                                                          determine the progress of implementing countermeasures and explain the obstacles and research directions.
                                                          Results: This paper covers the current threat landscape and the existing taxonomies of attack vectors on the
                                                          routing layer. By analyzing different taxonomies, we detected overlapping incident types. Therefore, a unified
                                                          and consolidated taxonomy is proposed to preserve consistency among different attack types, simultaneously
                                                          giving a more detailed breakdown of incident classification. This review also contains a comprehensive
                                                          comparative study of protective measures, including historical, current, and developing techniques. This study
                                                          includes the efficiency of proactive (prevention) and reactive (mitigation) practices and their caveats. The
                                                          authors also examine the most promising development plans for new and existing countermeasures.
                                                          Conclusion: Global implementation efforts are focused on routing security’s safeguard mechanisms based on
                                                          mutual protection, e.g., the Resource Public Key Infrastructure (RPKI) system. This determinant creates an
                                                          infinite regress problem known as the chicken or the egg—the primary dilemma. The authors find that the
                                                          point of critical mass is achieved but that RPKI still faces vital issues.

                                                                                            7.3. Adoption issues

                                                                                                Is routing security a chicken and egg situation? A singular AS
                                                                                            does not benefit directly from investing and deploying routing security
                                                                                            measures, as the security gain is distributed among all the networks on
                                                                                            the Internet. A decades-long but urgent need for security upgrades is a
                                                                                            collective action problem—the more network operators cooperate, the
                                                                                            higher the number of incident reductions and the lower impact can be
                                                                                            expected.
                                                                                            Lack of awareness
                                                                                                Since the RPKI launch in 2010, there have been countless cam-
                                                                                            paigns, lectures, and actions to popularize and encourage deploy-
                                                                                            ment among operators. The topic has been covered numerous times
                                                                                            [71,72,75,76] during lectures at Information Technology (IT) and
                                                                                            Information and Communications Technology (ICT) conferences. The
                                                                                            distinctive initiative is the MANRS program for ISPs. By introducing
                                                                                            generally agreed norms, MANRS has taken a big step in reducing the
                                                                                            most critical routing threats [77]. Overall, given the several years of
                                                                                            dissemination campaigns, lack of awareness should be excluded from
                                                                                            deployment progress obstacles.
                                                                                            Little security benefit
                                                                                                Researchers who were surveyed [9] network operators from NANOG
                                                                                            and RIPE NCC communities pointed out that ‘‘deployment lags mainly
                                                                                            due to RPKI’s limited adoption and little security benefits, but also
                                                                                            due to the increased CAPEX and OPEX costs and increased complexity
